The modular kitchen is typically the single most expensive item in a home interior project — and the one where cost estimation is most confusing. Contractors quote in running feet, square feet, or lump sums, making comparison nearly impossible without a reference guide.

This guide gives you per-running-foot prices for every major material type, city-wise rate variations, and a clear breakdown of what drives kitchen costs up or down.

5 Ways to Save on Your Modular Kitchen

  • Choose laminate over acrylic: Laminate costs 40–50% less than acrylic on the same plywood carcass and is more heat-resistant. Modern laminate designs are indistinguishable from acrylic at normal viewing distance.
  • Use Indian granite instead of quartz countertop: Indian black/brown granite at ₹400–600/RFT performs as well as quartz at ₹1,500–2,000/RFT for most residential kitchens.
  • Buy hardware directly: Hinges and drawer channels bought from Chickpet (Bangalore), Lohar Chawl (Mumbai), or Amazon save ₹200–400 per piece vs contractor supply.
  • Simplify the layout: Straight kitchen = lowest cost. L-shape = moderate. U-shape and island configurations add 25–40% to cost for the same RFT due to corner units, extra installation complexity, and wasted material.
  • Skip the loft shutters: Open shelves for the top 12 inches of wall cabinets look contemporary and save ₹5,000–10,000 vs full loft shutters.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a modular kitchen cost in India in 2026?+

A modular kitchen in India costs ₹60,000–4 lakh+ depending on size, material, and city. A standard 10 running feet kitchen with BWR plywood carcass and laminate shutters costs ₹95,000–1,40,000 in metro cities. Acrylic shutters cost 50–70% more; premium PU or glass options start at ₹1,80,000 for the same size.

What is the best material for modular kitchen shutters in India?+

Laminate on 18mm BWR plywood offers the best value for most Indian homes — durable, moisture-resistant, easy to maintain, and available in hundreds of finishes. Acrylic looks better initially but can scratch and yellow over time. PU finish is the best premium option. For high-budget kitchens, lacquered glass or handleless aluminium profiles offer a contemporary look.

What is a running foot in kitchen measurement?+

A running foot (RFT) is a linear measurement of kitchen cabinets. It refers to one foot of cabinet width. A typical 10 RFT kitchen includes both base (below countertop) and wall cabinets over 10 linear feet. Most 2BHK kitchens in India are 8–14 running feet; 3BHK kitchens run 12–20 feet.

Is it better to buy a modular kitchen from a brand or a local carpenter?+

Factory-made modular kitchens from brands (Häfele, HomeLane, Livspace) offer better finish quality, warranty, and precise manufacturing. Local carpenters can be 20–35% cheaper but quality depends heavily on the individual carpenter's skill. For kitchens above ₹1.5 lakh, a factory-made modular unit is usually worth the premium.

How long does modular kitchen installation take?+

A factory-made modular kitchen takes 21–28 days to manufacture after design approval, plus 1–2 days for installation. On-site carpenter-built kitchens take 15–25 days for a 10 RFT kitchen. Add 3–5 days for electrical work (exhaust, chimney point) and plumbing adjustments if needed.
