How to Evaluate a Contractor Quote in India

Most Indian homeowners accept the first contractor quote they receive. That's a mistake that costs an average of ₹1.5–4 lakh on a typical construction project. The problem isn't dishonest contractors — it's the information asymmetry. Contractors quote dozens of projects a year; homeowners build once or twice in a lifetime. This tool helps level that playing field.

Step 1: Get the Rate Per Sqft — Then Compare

Always ask for the rate per built-up sqft. This single number tells you more than any other figure in a contractor quote. Compare it to the benchmarks above for your city and quality grade. A rate within ±10% of the fair market rate is acceptable. More than 15% above? Push back or get competing quotes.

Step 2: Demand an Itemised Quote

Never accept a single-line lump-sum quote. A proper contractor quote should include separate line items for: site preparation and excavation, foundation and plinth, structural concrete and steel, brick/block masonry, plastering (internal and external), flooring material and laying, electrical (wiring, points, switches), plumbing (pipes, fittings, sanitary ware), doors and windows, painting, and contingency. Any contractor who refuses to itemise is hiding something.

Red Flags in Contractor Quotes

  • No specification of material grades — "good quality tiles" tells you nothing. Ask for brand, grade, and sqft price.
  • Unusually low total with large extras — Some contractors win contracts with low base quotes, then charge separately for "extra" items that any reasonable person would consider included.
  • Demanding more than 30% upfront — Legitimate contractors don't need 50% advance. High advance demands are a risk signal.
  • No written agreement — Never start work without a signed, itemised contract with a payment schedule and delay penalty clauses.
  • No GST registration — Construction services above ₹20 lakh require GST registration. Ask for the GST number and verify it at the GST portal.

The 30/30/30/10 Payment Rule

Always structure payments around milestones, never time periods. The recommended structure: 30% on signing and material procurement, 30% on structural completion (roof slab cast), 30% on finishing commencement (plastering, flooring start), 10% final payment only after you've done a full walkthrough and all snags are fixed. This structure keeps the contractor motivated throughout and protects you if they default mid-project.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if my contractor's rate per sqft is fair?+

Compare the quoted rate to current market benchmarks for your city and quality grade. Economy construction in metros should cost ₹1,400–1,600/sqft, standard ₹1,900–2,100/sqft, premium ₹2,800–3,200/sqft. If the quote is more than 20% above these ranges, ask for an itemised breakdown and get at least one more quote.

What is a reasonable markup for a contractor on materials?+

A reasonable contractor markup on materials is 10–15%. Anything above 20% is excessive. Ask your contractor for material invoices, or better yet, buy key materials (cement, steel, tiles) directly from dealers and only pay the contractor for labour.

Should I be suspicious of very low contractor quotes?+

Yes. Unusually low quotes (more than 20–25% below market rates) almost always indicate either very low-quality materials, scope exclusions that will be added later as 'extras', or contractors who'll ask for large advance payments and disappear. Always verify what's included in a low quote item by item.

What payment structure should I insist on with a contractor?+

The safest payment structure is: 30% on contract signing, 30% when the structure (slab/columns) is complete, 30% when finishing begins, and the final 10% only after snag-free handover. Never pay more than 30% upfront. Avoid contractors who demand 50%+ advance.

What documents should I get from a contractor before starting?+

Insist on: a written, itemised quote with material specifications (grades, brands), a project timeline with milestones, a formal agreement with payment schedule and penalty clauses for delays, and the contractor's GST number and Aadhaar/PAN. Check references from 2–3 previous clients specifically.

Log Yeh Bhi Poochte Hain (People Also Ask)

Contractor quotes ke baare mein common sawaal

Contractor ne zyada quote diya hai — kaise negotiate karein?+

Seedha "rate kam karo" mat bolein — yeh weak negotiation hai. Iske bajaye: (1) 2–3 competing quotes lane ke baad specifically bolein "XYZ contractor ne ₹X/sqft quote diya hai same specification ke liye." (2) Itemised breakdown maangein — kahan markup zyada hai woh samjhein. (3) Materials khud buy karne ka offer dein (contractor sirf labour le). Yeh approach se 15–25% tak reduction ho sakta hai bina relationship kharaab kiye.

Contractor ko advance kitna dena chahiye?+

Safe payment structure: 30% signing pe (materials procurement ke liye), 30% slab/structure complete hone par, 30% finishing shuru hone par (plastering, flooring), 10% final handover ke baad sirf jab sab kuch sahi ho. 50% se zyada advance dene wala contractor red flag hai — legitimate contractor ko itne advance ki zarurat nahi hoti. Final 10% apna sabse powerful quality assurance tool hai.

Quote mein kya-kya included hona chahiye?+

Ek proper construction quote mein yeh sab alag-alag line items mein hona chahiye: excavation, foundation, RCC columns/beams (with steel grade — Fe500), brickwork (material specify hona chahiye), plastering (internal + external), flooring (brand, grade, sqft rate), electrical (wire brand — Finolex/Polycab, point count), plumbing (CPVC/UPVC pipes), doors/windows, painting (brand, coats). Agar "lump sum" quote aa raha hai toh itemised maangein — warna baad mein "extra" charge aata hai.

Contractor ka GST number check karna zaroori hai kya?+

Haan, ₹20 lakh se upar ke construction projects ke liye GST registration mandatory hai. Contractor ka GSTIN lein aur gst.gov.in par verify karein. Bina GST wala contractor ya toh chhota (less than ₹20L turnover) hai ya tax evade kar raha hai — dono cases mein aap legal protection se bahar ho sakte hain dispute ki situation mein. Invoice zaroor lein har payment ka.

Kya contractor reference check karna important hai?+

Bahut important — aur zyaadatar log skip karte hain. Sirf contractor ke diye hue references pe nahi — khud site visit karein aur previous clients se directly poochein: "Kya kaam time par hua?", "Quote se zyada kharcha hua?", "Material quality kaisi thi?", "Koi issue aa raha hai abhi bhi?" Aur specifically pichle 2 saal ke completed projects dekhein — purana portfolio misleading ho sakta hai.
