Home Interior Cost in India 2026 — What to Budget For

Interior renovation is the single most misunderstood cost category for Indian homeowners. Unlike construction, where per-sqft rates are relatively standardised, interior costs vary wildly — not just by city or quality, but by the scope definition. One contractor's "complete interior" might exclude bathrooms, flooring, and electrical work that another contractor includes by default.

This estimator helps you build a realistic budget before you talk to a single contractor, so you can evaluate every quote against an independent baseline.

How to Read Your Estimate

This estimator uses per-room base rates derived from current Bangalore, Mumbai, Delhi, and Pune market data. City factors adjust the rates: metro costs are benchmarked at 1.0×, Tier-1 at 0.88×, Tier-2 at 0.76×, Tier-3 at 0.65×. An 8% contingency is included for unforeseen work.

Use this estimate to set your negotiation floor. If a contractor quotes significantly below this estimate, probe the specification — cheaper quotes typically use lower-grade plywood, skip hardware brands, or exclude items from scope.

What Drives Interior Costs Up

  • Shutter material: Acrylic costs 50–70% more than laminate on the same plywood carcass. For most rooms, the visual difference is marginal after 3 years.
  • Hardware brand: Blum (Austrian) hardware costs 3× more than local brands. Hettich (German) is a strong mid-market choice at 1.5–2× local. For daily-use items like kitchen drawers, invest in Hettich or Blum.
  • False ceiling design: A plain gypsum ceiling costs ₹75–90/sqft. Adding cove lighting, levels, and curves can take this to ₹150–200/sqft for the same area.
  • Flooring: Large-format tiles (800×800 GVT) cost 2–3× more than standard 600×600 tiles in material cost alone, plus higher laying complexity.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does interior design cost for a 2BHK in India?+

A 2BHK interior in India costs ₹4.5–18 lakh depending on city and quality. Standard quality in metro cities (Bangalore, Mumbai) typically runs ₹7–10 lakh covering kitchen, wardrobes, living room, false ceiling, and painting.

What is included in a full home interior package?+

A full home interior package typically includes modular kitchen, bedroom wardrobes, TV unit/living room furniture, false ceiling in main areas, lighting, painting, and additional electrical points. Beds, sofas, dining tables, and curtains are usually separate furniture budgets.

Is it cheaper to get a contractor or interior designer in India?+

A local contractor is 15–25% cheaper than a full-service interior design firm. However, design firms handle procurement, project management, and quality control, which can save you time and prevent costly mistakes. For a 2BHK budget below ₹8 lakh, a good contractor works fine. Above ₹15 lakh, a designer often pays for themselves.

How long does full home interior work take?+

A 2BHK full interior typically takes 45–75 days from order to handover. A 3BHK takes 60–90 days. Modular units (kitchen, wardrobes) take 21–28 days to manufacture after design approval. Factor in 2–3 weeks for painting, electrical, and final touches.

What is the best plywood grade for home interiors in India?+

BWR (Boiling Water Resistant) 18mm grade plywood is the standard recommendation for Indian homes. It handles humidity well and is ISI-certified. MR (Moisture Resistant) grade is cheaper but not suitable for kitchens or bathrooms. Commercial brands: Greenply, CenturyPly, Kitply are reliable.

How can I save money on my home interior?+

Top savings: choose laminate shutters over acrylic (40–60% cheaper, negligible quality difference for most rooms), use PVC instead of gypsum for bathroom false ceilings, buy hardware (hinges, channels) directly from dealers, get 3 quotes, and skip false ceilings in bedrooms and utility areas.

Data Sources & Methodology

Interior cost estimates are based on material price data, contractor rate surveys, and industry association benchmarks. Rates represent mid-market costs — neither the cheapest local contractor nor the most expensive brand-name firm.

🪵 Plywood Price Index (IPA)

Indian Plywood Manufacturers Association (IPMA) publishes monthly plywood and panel product price data. BWR grade 18mm plywood — the standard for Indian interior work — is tracked for 6 major cities. Used to calibrate kitchen and wardrobe cost estimates.

ipma.in ↗

🎨 Paint Manufacturers Data

Painting cost estimates incorporate published MRP and trade pricing from Asian Paints, Berger, and Indigo — India's three largest paint manufacturers, who collectively publish quarterly price lists used by the trade. Rates include material + labour for 2 coats primer + 2 topcoats.

asianpaints.com ↗

🏠 CREDAI Interior Cost Survey

Confederation of Real Estate Developers' Associations of India (CREDAI) publishes periodic surveys on interior fit-out costs across major Indian cities. We use CREDAI city-wise data to set our city adjustment multipliers (metro × 1.0, Tier-1 × 0.88, Tier-2 × 0.76, Tier-3 × 0.65).

credai.org ↗

🚿 Sanitary Ware Price Data (ISWAI)

Indian Sanitary Ware & Allied Industries Association publishes manufacturer price lists for bathroom fittings. Bathroom cost estimates use ISWAI mid-grade product pricing (Jaquar, Hindware, Cera equivalent) as the standard quality benchmark.

iswai.org ↗

📋 NSDC Skill Rate Surveys

National Skill Development Corporation publishes wage rate data for interior trade workers — carpenters, painters, electricians, false ceiling installers — across cities. Labour component of estimates uses NSDC data adjusted for current market conditions.

nsdcindia.org ↗

Estimates cross-validated against 200+ homeowner-submitted interior quotes collected between January 2025 and March 2026 across Mumbai, Bangalore, Pune, Hyderabad, Delhi NCR, and Chennai. Outliers excluded; median-band rates used.

Log Yeh Bhi Poochte Hain (People Also Ask)

Interior ke baare mein sabse common sawaal

2 BHK ka interior kitne mein hota hai?+

Metro city mein 2BHK ka complete interior (modular kitchen, wardrobes, TV unit, false ceiling, painting) ka kharcha hota hai: Basic quality: ₹3.5–5 lakh | Standard quality: ₹7–10 lakh | Premium quality: ₹12–18 lakh. Bangalore aur Mumbai mein slightly zyada, Pune aur Hyderabad mein thoda kam. Upar ka estimator select karein BHK type aur city — exact room-wise breakdown milega.

Modular kitchen mein kitna kharcha aata hai?+

10 running feet ki standard modular kitchen ka kharcha (metro city, BWR plywood + laminate shutters, granite countertop): ₹95,000–1,40,000. Acrylic shutters choose karein toh ₹1,40,000–2,20,000. Laminate aur acrylic ka actual difference bahut kam hota hai daily use mein — paise bachana hai toh laminate hi lein. Hardware (hinges, channels) khud Hettich ya Ebco se khareedein contractor ke through nahi.

Interior designer hire karein ya seedha contractor se karwayen?+

₹8 lakh se kam budget mein: ek achha contractor + aapke reference images = sufficient. ₹15 lakh se upar: interior designer (jo 10–15% charge karta hai project cost ka) usually apni fee se zyada bachat karwa deta hai — better material sourcing, quality supervision, aur design consistency ki wajah se. Always 2–3 contractors ke quotes lein aur portfolio dekhein before finalising.

Plywood mein BWR aur MR ka kya farak hai?+

BWR (Boiling Water Resistant) — ISI-marked, moisture resistant, kitchen aur bathroom ke liye suitable. MR (Moisture Resistant) — cheaper but warps in humid conditions, kitchen ke liye unsuitable. Koi bhi contractor MR plywood quote mein dal ke same rate charge kar sakta hai — delivery par ISI certificate aur stamp check zaroor karein. Greenply, CenturyPly, Kitply reliable brands hain.

Interior ka kaam kitne time mein hota hai?+

2BHK ka complete interior: 45–75 din. Modular kitchen aur wardrobes factory mein 21–28 din mein bante hain design approval ke baad. Painting aur electrical work ke liye 2–3 hafte aur. Timeline tight rakhen: contractor se milestone-based schedule lena — otherwise kaam stretch hota rehta hai. Final 10% payment sirf sab kuch punch-list free hone ke baad dein.
