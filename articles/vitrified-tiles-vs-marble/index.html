The flooring decision is permanent (or at least semi-permanent) — and it significantly affects your home's resale value, maintenance burden, and aesthetic. The vitrified tiles vs marble debate is one of the most common questions Indian homeowners face, and the answer isn't simply "which looks better."

This guide gives you the real total cost comparison — material, laying, polishing, and long-term maintenance — to help you make a financially sound decision.

Bottom Line

  • For most Indian homes, premium GVT vitrified tiles (800×800, ₹115–190/sqft installed) offer the best combination of aesthetics, durability, and value. Reserve marble for the living room if budget allows.
  • The 10-year total cost of marble flooring is 2–5× that of vitrified tiles. Unless resale value or personal preference justifies it, high-quality vitrified tiles are the practical choice for most rooms.
  • Kitchen and bathrooms should always have anti-skid textured tiles — neither polished marble nor high-gloss vitrified tiles are safe when wet.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is cheaper — vitrified tiles or marble flooring in India?+

Vitrified tiles are generally 30–70% cheaper than Indian marble and 300–500% cheaper than Italian marble. Standard vitrified tiles cost ₹45–90/sqft (material) while Indian marble costs ₹80–180/sqft and Italian marble ₹300–600/sqft. However, marble's long-term maintenance costs (polishing every 3–5 years) partially close this gap.

What is the difference between GVT, DGVT, and Double Charge vitrified tiles?+

GVT (Glazed Vitrified Tiles) have a printed design on a glass-like surface — most modern tiles are GVT. DGVT (Double Glazed Vitrified Tiles) have an additional protective layer and better scratch resistance. Double Charge tiles have the design pressed 3–4mm deep, making them more wear-resistant but with limited design options. For residential use, GVT or DGVT are best.

Can marble flooring be used in all rooms?+

Indian marble is suitable for living rooms, bedrooms, and foyers. Avoid marble in kitchens (stains easily from oil and acids), bathrooms (slippery when wet, needs non-slip treatment), and outdoor areas (weathers quickly). Vitrified tiles with anti-skid texture are safer and more practical for kitchens, bathrooms, and balconies.

How often does marble flooring need polishing in India?+

In Indian climate conditions, marble typically needs polishing every 3–5 years. Urban areas with harder water may require more frequent polishing due to mineral deposits. Each polishing session costs ₹8–15/sqft depending on the city and condition. Over 10 years, marble polishing adds ₹20,000–40,000 to your flooring cost on a 1,000 sqft home.

What large format tile sizes are available in India?+

Indian tile manufacturers now produce tiles up to 1200×2400mm (Slab size). Most popular residential sizes: 600×600mm (most affordable), 800×800mm (premium appearance), 1200×600mm (plank format), and 1200×1200mm (large format). Larger tiles require flatter floors (laser levelling often needed), cost more to lay, and generate more wastage.
