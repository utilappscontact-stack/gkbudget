Union Budget 2026-27 was presented in February 2026 with a strong focus on infrastructure, affordable housing, and middle-class tax relief. For homeowners planning construction or renovation in the April 2026 – March 2027 financial year, this budget has specific implications for costs, loan availability, and government subsidies.

This guide cuts through the budget noise and identifies the specific provisions that will affect your construction or renovation costs — with concrete numbers.

GST on Construction — What You Actually Pay

GST on construction remains unchanged in Budget 2026-27. Here's the exact structure:

Transaction TypeGST RateNotes
Construction services (works contract)18%What you pay a contractor for services
Affordable housing (under PMAY)5%Carpet area ≤60 sqm metro, ≤90 sqm elsewhere
Under-construction apartment purchase5%No ITC benefit to developer
Ready-to-move apartment with OCExemptNot a supply of service, no GST
Construction materials (cement)28%GST on cement remains highest in the world
Construction materials (steel, tiles)18%On TMT bars, vitrified tiles

Key takeaway on GST: The 28% GST on cement is a significant contributor to construction costs. A ₹1 lakh construction project uses approximately 350–400 bags of cement; the GST component alone adds ₹30,000–40,000 to material costs at this rate. Industry lobbying to reduce cement GST to 18% continues, but no change was made in Budget 2026-27.

PMAY-Urban 2.0 — Eligibility and Benefit in 2026

PMAY-Urban 2.0 remains active with continued central government allocation. If you're planning to take a home loan and your household income qualifies, the subsidy significantly reduces your effective interest cost:

The subsidy is credited to your loan account upfront (within 3–4 months of disbursement), directly reducing your outstanding principal. For MIG-I buyers, a ₹2.35 lakh subsidy on a ₹40 lakh loan saves approximately ₹580/month in EMI.

Material Price Outlook — FY 2026-27

Budget 2026-27's massive infrastructure capex allocation will increase institutional demand for steel and cement, creating upward pressure on material prices for private construction:

  • Cement: Expected 4–7% price increase in FY 2026-27. Current: ₹370–420/bag (OPC 53 grade metro). Forecast: ₹390–450/bag by Q3 2026.
  • TMT Steel (Fe500): Expected 5–8% increase. Current: ₹62,000–68,000/MT. Infrastructure demand is the key driver.
  • Labour: 8–12% annual increase expected, driven by MGNREGA competition and infrastructure project demand for skilled labour.
  • Tiles and sanitary ware: Relatively stable — expect 3–5% increase tracking general inflation.

What Should Homeowners Do Before April 2026?

  • Lock in contractor contracts now — sign contracts and fix material prices before April 2026 to lock in current rates. Include a material price escalation cap clause.
  • Buy structural materials immediately — if you have storage space, buying 3–6 months' worth of cement and steel at current prices is a practical hedge against the anticipated 4–8% Q2 2026 increases.
  • Check PMAY eligibility — if your household income is below ₹12 lakh/year, apply for PMAY subsidy before your loan disbursement. The subsidy scheme has limited annual quotas.
  • Choose old vs new tax regime carefully — if you're taking a significant home loan (above ₹25 lakh), calculate whether the old tax regime with housing deductions gives you more benefit than the new regime's lower slab rates.

Bottom Line

  • Budget 2026-27 doesn't dramatically change construction economics, but the infrastructure capex push will increase material costs by 4–8% through the year — locking in prices now saves real money.
  • PMAY-Urban 2.0 subsidy of ₹2.3–2.67 lakh is a genuine benefit for EWS/LIG/MIG-I households — check eligibility before your loan disbursement.
  • GST on cement at 28% remains a significant and regressive cost on construction. No relief in this budget — factor the full GST into your material cost estimates.

Frequently Asked Questions

How will Budget 2026-27 affect home construction costs in India?+

Union Budget 2026-27 (announced February 2026) has several direct impacts: continued PMAY-Urban 2.0 funding boosts affordable housing supply, no change to GST on construction services (still 18% for construction services, 5% for affordable housing), and infrastructure spending increases which could modestly increase steel and cement demand, putting upward pressure on material prices.

Has GST on home construction changed in 2026?+

No change in GST structure for residential construction as of Budget 2026-27. Construction of a complex/building/civil structure: 18% GST on services. Affordable residential apartments (PMAY-eligible, carpet area ≤60 sqm in metros, ≤90 sqm elsewhere): 5% GST. Under-construction residential apartments: 5% (with ITC restrictions). Ready-to-move properties with OC: exempt from GST.

What is PMAY-Urban 2.0 and who is eligible?+

PMAY-Urban 2.0 (Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana) provides interest subsidy on home loans for EWS (annual income below ₹3L), LIG (₹3–6L), and MIG-I (₹6–12L) categories. MIG-II (₹12–18L) was covered under PMAY 1.0 but has limited coverage under 2.0. The subsidy is credited upfront to the loan account, reducing effective interest cost by ₹2.3–2.67 lakh depending on category.

Will cement and steel prices increase in 2026 due to budget allocations?+

Infrastructure spending in Budget 2026-27 (₹11.11 lakh crore capex) will increase institutional demand for cement and steel, potentially adding 3–5% to material costs for private construction. This is in addition to the 6–8% annual price escalation already factored into 2026 rates. Lock in material prices via price-lock agreements with dealers before starting structural work.

What income tax benefits are available for home buyers in 2026?+

Under current tax rules: Section 24(b) allows deduction of up to ₹2 lakh/year on home loan interest for self-occupied property. Section 80C allows deduction of up to ₹1.5 lakh for principal repayment. Under the new tax regime (which is now default), these deductions are not available except for let-out property. First-time buyers under PMAY get additional benefits through the subsidy scheme.
