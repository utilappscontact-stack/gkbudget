GHMC iPass — One of India's Fastest Approvals

Hyderabad's GHMC iPass system is a significant advantage for builders. For residential plots up to 250 sqm, approvals are granted within 3–7 working days online. Key points:

  • Self-certification system: Licensed architect certifies compliance; GHMC approves based on this certification for small plots
  • Development charges: ₹250–450/sqft depending on zone and use type
  • HMDA approval: Peripheral areas take 3–8 weeks — still faster than most Indian metros
  • Layout approval (LRS): If your plot doesn't have layout approval, you'll need LRS (Layout Regularisation Scheme) — check status before buying

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the construction cost per sqft in Hyderabad in 2026?+

In Hyderabad, construction costs range from ₹1,350/sqft (Economy) to ₹2,600/sqft (Premium) in 2026. Standard quality construction typically costs ₹1,800–2,000/sqft. Hyderabad offers good value compared to Bangalore and Mumbai — the city has a competitive contractor market and good material availability.

What is the difference between GHMC and HMDA jurisdiction in Hyderabad?+

GHMC (Greater Hyderabad Municipal Corporation) covers the 650 sq km core city area including areas like Kukatpally, LB Nagar, Uppal, Secunderabad, and Jubilee Hills. HMDA (Hyderabad Metropolitan Development Authority) oversees peripheral areas like Shamshabad, Ghatkesar, Medchal, Shankarpally, and Chevella. HMDA areas are more affordable to build in, with lower development charges.

Why is Hyderabad construction cheaper than Bangalore?+

Hyderabad has lower construction costs than Bangalore for several reasons: lower labour wages (masons earn ₹700–950/day vs ₹800–1,100/day in Bangalore), better supply chain for materials (Telangana has cement plants reducing transport costs), a competitive contractor market with high project volume from IT corridor growth, and relatively lower regulatory burden.

What are the key differences between building in Gachibowli vs Kompally vs Shamshabad?+

Gachibowli (IT corridor) has the highest construction rates due to premium demand — ₹1,900–2,100/sqft standard. Kompally (North Hyderabad) is mid-range at ₹1,700–1,900/sqft standard. Shamshabad (South, near airport) is growing fast and costs ₹1,500–1,750/sqft standard. HMDA peripheral zones are 15–25% cheaper than GHMC core areas.

How long does GHMC building plan approval take in Hyderabad?+

GHMC building plan approval for residential projects (under 250 sqm) typically takes 3–7 working days through the online iPass (Integrated Permission Approval and Self-Certification System) — one of the fastest approval systems in India. Larger projects and those requiring environmental clearance take 4–12 weeks. HMDA approvals take 3–8 weeks.
