Frequently Asked Questions

What is the construction cost per sqft in Bangalore in 2026?+

In Bangalore, construction costs range from ₹1,400/sqft (Economy) to ₹2,800/sqft (Premium) in 2026. Standard quality costs ₹1,900–2,100/sqft. Tech corridors like Whitefield and Koramangala command 10–15% above average.

Why are Bangalore construction costs rising in 2026?+

Costs rose 8–10% due to: skilled labour migration post-COVID, M-sand price increases from Karnataka river sand restrictions, rising wages in the competitive tech city labour market, and strong housing demand from IT/ITES growth.

What is the BBMP approval process?+

BBMP requires a Building Plan Approval (BPA) + commencement certificate via the BBMP online portal. Timeline: 2–5 months. Budget ₹50,000–2 lakh for approval costs depending on plot size.

Is panchayat limit cheaper than BBMP?+

Yes — avoids BBMP development charges saving ₹25–50/sqft. However, lower resale liquidity and limited civic services are downsides. For long-term owner-occupied homes, BBMP or BDA layouts recommended.

What are material prices in Bangalore Q1 2026?+

OPC 53 cement ₹360–400/bag (ACC, Ramco). TMT steel Fe500 ₹60,000–66,000/MT. Robo sand ₹40–55/sqft — standard since Karnataka banned river sand. AAC blocks ₹38–50/block.
