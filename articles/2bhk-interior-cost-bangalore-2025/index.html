You've got the keys. The builder handover is done. Now comes the part nobody prepares you for: figuring out how much it'll actually cost to make your Bangalore 2BHK liveable.

Interior contractors in Bangalore will quote you anywhere from ₹4 lakh to ₹22 lakh for the same 2BHK apartment. The variance isn't random — it's a function of materials, quality of woodwork, brand of hardware, and what's included in the scope. This guide cuts through the noise and gives you real, current numbers from the Bangalore market so you can evaluate every quote on your desk.

What Does a 2BHK Interior Include?

Before looking at costs, it's essential to define scope. A "complete 2BHK interior" in Bangalore typically covers:

  • Modular kitchen (base + wall cabinets, countertop, fittings)
  • Wardrobes for both bedrooms (sliding or hinged, with internals)
  • Living room: TV unit, possibly a crockery unit or bookshelf
  • False ceiling (usually living room + master bedroom)
  • Electrical work: additional points, concealed wiring, light fittings
  • Painting (emulsion or texture)
  • Flooring (if different from builder-provided, or upgrades)

Not typically included: Sofas, beds, dining tables, washing machine area, curtains, and decorative items. These are separate furniture budgets.

3. Living Room: TV Unit + Storage

A basic TV unit with storage in a Bangalore 2BHK costs ₹25,000–60,000. A full wall unit with crockery and display sections runs ₹65,000–1,20,000. If your living room is compact (below 160 sqft), a floating TV unit (₹20,000–35,000) is often the smartest choice.

4. False Ceiling

False ceilings have become almost standard in Bangalore apartments. Gypsum board ceilings cost ₹75–110/sqft in Bangalore including labour. POP (Plaster of Paris) is slightly cheaper at ₹55–80/sqft but less durable. For a typical 2BHK, false ceiling in living room + master bedroom covers roughly 350–450 sqft.

5. Painting

Painting costs in Bangalore are quoted per sqft of wall area. A standard 2BHK has approximately 1,000–1,200 sqft of paintable wall area. Premium emulsion (Asian Paints Royale, Berger Silk) costs ₹14–22/sqft for 2 coats including putty and primer. Economy paints (Tractor Emulsion, Ace) cost ₹8–13/sqft.

6. Electrical Work

If your builder has provided basic electrical points, you'll still need additional work: more light points for false ceiling fixtures, fan points, AC connection points (if not provided), and modular switch upgrades. Budget ₹20,000–45,000 for electrical additions in a standard Bangalore 2BHK. If you're redoing all switches to premium brands (Legrand, Anchor Roma), add ₹15,000–25,000.

How to Negotiate with Your Interior Contractor in Bangalore

  • Always get 3 quotes: Interior prices in Bangalore vary 20–35% between contractors for identical specifications. Never accept the first quote.
  • Provide a written specification: Vague briefs lead to vague quotes. Specify plywood grade (18mm BWR), shutter material (laminate/acrylic), hardware brand (Hettich/Ebco/Blum), and countertop material.
  • Buy hardware yourself: Hinges, drawer channels, and handles are massively marked up by contractors. Buy directly from dealers in Bangalore's Chickpet or online.
  • Verify plywood grade on-site: Ask for the ISI-marked plywood invoice. Some contractors substitute MR-grade plywood (which warps in humidity) for the BWR grade you've specified.
  • Don't pay more than 30% upfront: Structure payments as 30% on order, 30% on material delivery, 30% on completion, 10% on final snag-free handover.

Bottom Line

  • A standard 2BHK interior in Bangalore costs ₹4.7–7 lakh for execution-only work in 2025–26. Full design + execution runs ₹7–10 lakh for mid-range quality.
  • The kitchen and wardrobes together account for 45–55% of the total interior budget — focus your negotiation energy here.
  • Always specify materials in writing, get 3 quotes, and pay in tranches. This alone saves most homeowners ₹50,000–1.5 lakh on an average project.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a 2BHK interior cost in Bangalore in 2025?+

A 2BHK interior in Bangalore costs between ₹4.5 lakh (basic) and ₹18 lakh (premium) in 2025. A standard mid-range 2BHK interior covering kitchen, wardrobes, TV unit, false ceiling, and painting typically costs ₹7–10 lakh all-in.

What is included in a 2BHK interior package in Bangalore?+

A typical package includes modular kitchen, bedroom wardrobes, TV unit, false ceiling (usually living room + master bedroom), electrical additions, and painting. Furniture (beds, sofas, dining tables), curtains, and décor are typically separate budgets.

How long does 2BHK interior work take in Bangalore?+

A full 2BHK interior takes 45–75 days once design is finalised and materials are ordered. Factory-made modular units take 3–4 weeks to deliver. Budget 2–3 extra weeks for painting, electrical work, and punch-list resolution.

Should I hire an interior designer or go direct to a contractor?+

For a standard 2BHK, a good execution contractor with reference images works fine. If your home has complex layouts or you want a cohesive design language throughout, an interior designer charging 10–15% of project cost can be worth it. Always check their portfolio and past client references in Bangalore specifically.

How can I reduce my 2BHK interior cost in Bangalore?+

Choose BWR plywood + laminate over acrylic, buy hardware directly from dealers, get 3 quotes minimum, opt for a simple L-shaped kitchen instead of complex island layouts, and skip false ceilings in bedrooms and bathrooms. These choices together can save ₹1–2.5 lakh on a standard project.
