How to Get & Compare Contractor Quotes in India — The Right Way

Getting contractor quotes in India is not like buying a product online where you compare prices on a screen. It requires preparation, the right questions, and the ability to compare quotes that are all structured differently. This guide gives you a systematic process — from finding candidates to signing an agreement — that levels the information gap between you and a contractor with 20 years of experience.

Know your number first

Before approaching any contractor, use our Construction Cost Calculator to get your baseline estimate. You should know what standard quality in your city costs per sqft before you hear anyone else’s number. This stops you from anchoring on the first quote you receive.

Step 1 — How to Find Contractors Worth Quoting

The worst way to find a contractor is an online directory. The best way is a warm referral from someone who recently built. Here are the sources ranked by reliability:

1
Referral from someone who recently built in your city
Ask friends, relatives, and colleagues who built in the last 2 years. Ask specifically: "Would you hire the same contractor again?" Not just "was the house good?" — because most people won’t admit they made a bad choice. Post in local WhatsApp groups and housing society chats. This is the single most reliable method.
2
Your architect or structural engineer
If you have already engaged an architect, ask them. Architects work with contractors constantly and know who delivers on time and on specification. They have reputational skin in the game — they won’t recommend someone who will embarrass them.
3
Local building material dealers
Visit the cement and steel dealers in your area. Ask which contractors buy regularly and pay on time. Dealers know exactly who buys quality materials and who buys the cheapest option. A contractor who buys OPC 53 consistently is different from one who buys the cheapest brand every time.
4
Online platforms — with caution
Platforms like NoBroker, UrbanClap/Urban Company, and Housing.com have contractor listings. These are reasonable for getting contact information, but do your own verification before trusting reviews on these platforms. Always visit a completed project in person before shortlisting.
5
Visiting active construction sites near your plot
If you see construction underway on a nearby plot, talk to the owner. Ask who their contractor is and whether they’re happy. You can also talk directly to the site supervisor working there and ask for the main contractor’s details. This is how many owner-builders find their best candidates.

Step 2 — What to Send Contractors (Your RFQ)

Don’t just call a contractor and ask "how much will it cost?" Send a written Request for Quote (RFQ) to every contractor you approach. This forces them to quote on the same scope and makes comparison possible.

Your RFQ should include:

  • Plot size and location (e.g. 30x40 ft plot, Koramangala, Bangalore)
  • Number of floors (G+1, G+2 etc.)
  • Total built-up area in sqft (use our calculator for this)
  • Quality grade required (economy / standard / premium — be specific about key items: cement brand, steel grade, brick type)
  • What is included (civil structure, basic electrification, plumbing, painting, flooring)
  • What is excluded (list anything you’re handling separately)
  • Expected timeline (your target start and completion)
  • Quote format requested (lump sum per sqft, OR itemised BOQ)
Request an itemised BOQ, not just a per-sqft rate

A Bill of Quantities breaks down every line item: foundation, columns, slabs, walls, plaster, flooring etc. with quantities and unit rates. This takes the contractor more time to prepare but makes cheating nearly impossible — they can’t inflate one line item to hide it in a lump sum total.

Red Flags — Walk Away If You See These

Quote significantly lower than all others

If one quote is 25%+ cheaper than two others on identical scope, they are either missing inclusions, planning to use inferior materials, or will add costs later. Low-ball quotes are the #1 cause of construction disputes in India.

Demands more than 10% advance before starting

Legitimate contractors with good cash flow do not need a large upfront advance. Demanding 25–30% advance before breaking ground is how fly-by-night operators collect money and disappear, or how they fund another project at your expense.

Refuses to sign a written agreement

"We work on trust" is not good enough for a ₹40 lakh project. Any contractor who refuses a written contract with payment milestones is protecting their ability to change terms later. No written agreement = no legal recourse.

Cannot provide references from projects in last 2 years

A contractor without recent references either has not been working or has unhappy clients. Ask for 2–3 names and actually call them. Visit at least one completed project if possible.

Vague or verbal material specifications

"Good quality" cement and "standard" steel are not specifications. If the quote does not name brands and grades, you have no basis for disputes when they install the cheapest alternative available.

Green Flags — Signs of a Trustworthy Contractor

Brings a site visit before quoting

A contractor who visits your plot before quoting understands your soil conditions, access, and site-specific challenges. Quotes prepared without a site visit are guesses.

Quotes milestone-based payment schedule

Foundation complete, slab cast, walls up, roof done, finishing complete — each stage tied to a verifiable inspection. This is how professional contractors structure payments because it protects both sides.

Suggests you independently verify measurements

A confident contractor welcomes independent measurement verification. If they encourage you to hire a third party to check work quality at each stage, that is a strong signal of good faith.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many contractor quotes should I get?+

Get at least 3 quotes. The spread between lowest and highest for identical specifications is typically 15–30% in India. With 2 quotes you might think the cheaper one is a bargain — with 3 you can identify the outlier. For projects above ₹50 lakh, get 4–5 quotes.

What should a contractor quote include?+

A proper quote should include: built-up area in sqft, material specifications (cement brand and grade, steel grade, brick type), labour rates, payment schedule tied to milestones, timeline with start and completion dates, explicit list of exclusions, and a contingency note. Any quote missing material specifications cannot be fairly compared.

Is it better to hire a contractor on labour-only or turnkey basis?+

Labour-only (you buy materials) saves 15–25% but requires significant time investment. Turnkey is more convenient but has higher markup risk. A hybrid approach works well for most owner-builders: turnkey for civil structure (foundation through roof), labour-only for finishing work (tiles, paint, fixtures) where you can specify and buy materials directly to control quality.

How much advance should I pay?+

Never more than 10% of total project value upfront. Tie all subsequent payments to verified construction milestones. Keep the final 10% until after your independent inspection confirms all punch list items are resolved. A contractor demanding 25–30% advance before starting is a significant red flag.

What should a construction agreement contain?+

Key clauses: total project cost and payment schedule, detailed material specifications, start and completion dates with penalty for delays (typically 0.5% of project value per week after grace period), dispute resolution mechanism, what happens if materials are substituted without approval, and clear definition of what constitutes project completion for final payment.
